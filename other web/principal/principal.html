From Principal's Desk
Principal Sr. Saramma Polisetty
When I look out at Loyola, I see many different institutions. There is the one where Kids with academic interest excel, another where athletes compete, and another where debaters succeed, scientists analyze, artist design, writers create and sports men and women emerge. It is in this catholic institution which is name honor of the sixteenth century St. Ignatius of Loyola, the young children are formed to become the pride of the family, responsible citizen of the country and intellectual innovative minds of the world. The Loyalties are indeed The Loyalties, the present children and Future men and women of conscience and Character. The beauty of Loyola school is in its homely formation of students with moral, intellectual and social values. While the state-of-the-art facilitates every academic pursuit, the priority is genuine human formation. Loyola's legacy is integral to its future as is our new generation of students. We continually forest the principles of discipline, order, the paramount importance of self-direction, the necessity of clear goals and objectives. Our school conducts itself in an atmosphere of professional inquiry, respect for individual, mutual trust, and Christian charity. Therefore, we strive to personal attention to ever single boy and girl. Each child is uniquely given the opportunity for optimal intellectual, social, emotional and physical development. Our institution sees in its youthful charges a teeming potential for goodness, a joyful enthusiasm, justice, goodness, truth, and beauty. Students are encouraged to develop the capacities for independent, critical judgment and creative expression. Training them to be life-long learners, responsible citizens and effective communicators are our key contribution.
